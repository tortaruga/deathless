Our philosophy

In his story 'The Silence of the Sirens', Kafka retells the episode of Ulysses and the mermaids: he writes that when they saw him tied to the ship-mast, with wax in his ears and his eyes radiant, they forgot to sing. They stared after him with their hair flowing in the wind and their expressions enraptured, petrified and voiceless.

He would have begged, please, please, torture me with your song, rip me off myself and call me to your sweet oblivion, grant me one mere second of immortality, for to die with your voice in my ears is to know no death at all. He would have crushed his skull against the rocks for the unbearable weight of that silence, the impossible stillness of an echo that refuses to answer back.

In one single note death will be obliterated. For one perfect, tiny moment that will stretch into eternity, the last moment before the waves welcome you home, you will be deathless.
